Who Are We?

Terrasave International is a non-governmental human rights agency whose primary function is to come to the aid of victims of chemical, biological and radiological terrorism and well as pharmaceutical disasters with an overall goal to eliminate these threats entirely. It was founded as a direct result of the tragedy in Raccoon City, Michigan when it became clear that governments and corporations were willing to cover up man-made disasters in order to gain profit.

Itâ€™s composed of researchers, scientists and professionals in various relevant fields, such as medical personnel. However, a vast majority of their ranks are made up of normal civilians, a number of which have been extensively trained to provide appropriate aid in times of these disasters. Many of them are victims of such atrocities themselves and have since dedicated their lives to the fight against them.

TerraSave first began as a small endeavor in 1998, operating out of a decommissioned middle school in Atlanta, Georgia to organize protests and pressue lawmakers within the United States.


It has grown significantly since then, moving its headquarters to a high-rise in Toronto, Ontario and expanding its scope to over 100 countries across the globe. TerraSave employs over 500 operatives spread between the North American HQ and four state-of-the-art regional facilities in Europe, Asia, West Africa and Oceania and even still, it continues to grow.

It has also begun working alongside other agencies, such as the World Health Organization and the United States Food and Drug Administration. The organization that TerraSave works in tandem with on a regular basis, however, is the Bioterrorism Security Assessment Alliance, the leading anti-bioterrorism force in the world.

Wherever the BSAA goes, TerraSave is sure to follow.

TerraSave International has overwhelming political and public support, but has made enemies out of pharmaceutical companies and some governmental bodies.
